Once Upon a Birthday

Two trajectories that, on paper, should never intersect. Two orbits separated by oceans, running in parallel like asymptotes approaching nothing.

Then time and space introduce a constraint — a funnel. When Light A arrives in Porto — not to stay, just 48 hours — the math breaks. For them to meet, the universe must solve an absurd equation: 230,000 people, one city, one timestamp, aligned free will.

0.00001% becomes 100% reality on one corner of one street.

Happy International Day of Robert, for correctly solving the most beautiful improbable proof.

Thank you very much!

Against all odds. Against common sense. Light A and Light B decided to export the anomaly to England.

There, they studied his favorite film, SpongeBob, as serious scholars of fine cinema. They ate fish and chips, wandered through the Harry Potter shops, and opened the Christmas season with me.

Shout out to Robert, the only man who can always find the route on his GPS… even when the probability says rerouting is inevitable.

Statistics say there’s only a 10% probability of talking after first-time meetups. Today, we’re almost at six months and we’re still not part of the statistic.

I hope we keep breaking statistics.
